About me

Hey, I'm Matt. Well Matthew Holder, but I go by Matt. I was in the navy for 4 years. I had a good time, met alot of great people, learned alot of cool things and had some truly amazing adventures. However, it was time to leave. After all I had only joined the navy so that I could find out who I wanted to be and what I wanted to do and I had found it. Read my mission statement to find out what I decided.

Mission statement

I don't like that so many people go without base necessities like food, water, shelter and medical care. For that reason, I've made it my life's mission to make these things easily accessable to as many people as I can possibly manage. I see robotics as the most efficient way to solve these problems, so my current goal is to learn to program responsive robots. I plan to accomplish this goal by October of 2021. My next goal will likely be changed by information learned along the way, but for now it is to learn to design and build actual robots for small tasks like dispensing items specified wirelessly. Although small, this goal allows me to dedicate larger amounts of time to full time employment; while still giving me the tools I need to move forward.

My Mantra

The grass is greenest where you water it. Someone mentioned this once in a zoom call and I very much like what it intends to say. Essentially it's talking about effort. It says that if you spend you're life wondering how to get to the places with the best grass you'll only end up jumping around from place to place never doing anything really significant. If you instead find one place that has the a high potential for good grass, water it, feed it, and trimit when needed you won't have to go anywhere for the best. You've already made it.
